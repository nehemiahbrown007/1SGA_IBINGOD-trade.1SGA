The Evolution of the Round

900 AD: The Origin

The first documented use of chemical propulsion—The Fire Lance. A transition from static defense to kinetic projection using bamboo and black powder.

1841: The Minié Ball

The introduction of the conical projectile. Hollow bases allowed expanding into rifling, increasing effective engagement distance by 300%.

1882: The Metal Jacket

Eduard Rubin’s copper jacket enabled high-velocity smokeless powder rounds, forming the baseline for all modern sovereign defense assets.
